# Vi — Piltover Enforcer

**Tier:** B · **Domains:** Fury / Order · **Win condition:** Conquer battlefields by dealing 3+ excess damage, chain Vi's legend exhaust to ready a unit immediately after each conquest, and snowball board control through repeated attacks in the same turn cycle before the opponent can stabilize.

## Mulligan

**Keep:**
- Vi - Destructive (ogn-036-298) — cheapest champion at 2 energy; Ganking lets her pressure multiple battlefields and her Recycle trash ability provides incremental Might scaling. Best early threat in the deck.
- Inferna (unl-002-219) — 2-energy Ambush unit with Assault 2; can be held and dropped as a Reaction mid-combat to surprise the opponent with extra damage, pushing you past the 3-excess threshold for Vi's legend trigger.
- Vault Breaker (unl-010-219) — the 1-energy spell that grants Assault 2 + Ganking; keep whenever you have a medium-sized attacker in hand, as it converts borderline attacks into guaranteed excess-damage conquests.
- Sharkling (unl-006-219) — 3 energy, Assault 4, Accelerate; if you can pay the Accelerate cost (1 energy + Fury rune) it enters ready and attacks immediately, setting up a turn-1 or turn-2 pressure window.
- Serrated Dirk (opp-009-221) — 1-energy equip (Fury rune only); attaches cheaply and boosts Might on any unit to make excess-damage math more reliable.

**Toss:**
- Towering Pairofant (unl-008-219) and Yeti Brawler (unl-018-219) — 6-energy threats that do nothing in the opening turns; snap-toss unless you have three cheaper units already in hand.
- Arena Kingpin (unl-001-219) — 5 energy, no Ambush; good mid-game pump engine but dead in an opening grip without early board presence.
- Right of Conquest (unl-015-219) — draw spell that scales with controlled battlefields; useless on turn 1 when you control nothing.
- Soul Harvest (unl-159-219) — situational removal that only hits 3 Might or less; valuable later but not an opener.
- Hextech Gauntlets (unl-188-219) — 3-energy gear whose cost is reduced by the equipped unit's Might; powerful once a large unit is out, clunky without one.

## Turn-by-turn game plan

### Turn 1 — Establish an attacker

Concrete line: Play Vi - Destructive (ogn-036-298) to your base for 2 energy. If you have a Fury rune, play Sharkling (unl-006-219) with Accelerate — pay 3 energy + 1 Fury rune to have it enter ready, then immediately move it to a battlefield. Alternatively play Inferna (unl-002-219) to hand (hold for Ambush reaction later). Equip Serrated Dirk (opp-009-221) with 1 Fury rune onto Vi - Destructive if held.

Intended outcome: You have a unit with Ganking (Vi - Destructive) or an already-ready attacker (Sharkling) in position by the end of turn 1, threatening a conquer as early as the first attack window.

### Turn 2 — First conquer attempt

Concrete line: Move Vi - Destructive or the Sharkling to a battlefield. Attack. If the path to excess damage looks narrow, play Vault Breaker (unl-010-219) for 1 energy + 1 Fury rune — grant the attacking unit Assault 2 and Ganking — pushing its effective Might high enough to kill all defenders and produce 3+ excess. If you conquered with 3+ excess, exhaust Vi - Piltover Enforcer (unl-187-219) to immediately ready the attacking unit. Use that readied unit's Ganking to move to a second battlefield and attack again.

Intended outcome: Up 1–2 points before the opponent has established their board. Vi's legend ability has fired at least once, demonstrating the loop.

### Turn 3 — Widen the board and threaten a second axis

Concrete line: Play Lord Broadmane (unl-012-219) as an Ambush Reaction if the opponent attacks into your battlefield (5 energy, gives all other units here Assault this turn). Or deploy Rengar - Unseen (unl-024-219) for 4 energy — he has Accelerate (enters ready), Assault 2, Deflect, and Ganking, making him nearly impossible to remove with a single spell and able to attack immediately. If the opponent pushed units at your battlefield last turn, drop Inferna mid-combat via Ambush. Activate Arena Kingpin's exhaust ability (+3 Might to a unit) if he is already on board.

Intended outcome: Multiple units across multiple battlefields. Opponent must defend at least two locations simultaneously; the excess-damage threshold becomes easier to hit because defenders are spread thin.

### Turn 4 — Chain conquers through Upstage Comedy and Square Up

Concrete line: Play Upstage Comedy (unl-009-219) for 2 energy to ready a unit that just conquered; repeat it for an additional 2 energy to ready a second unit. This lets you trigger Vi's legend ability and then use Upstage to fire a third attack in the same turn. Alternatively, cast Square Up (unl-017-219) for 4 energy — Assault 4 to a large unit — and use the Repeat cost (discard 1 + 4 energy) to give a second unit Assault 4. Deploy Towering Pairofant (unl-008-219) if a unit died this turn — it enters ready automatically.

Intended outcome: Three or more attacks resolved in one turn cycle. At least two battlefields conquered. Trapping Grounds (unl-217-219) generates a 1/1 Bird token with Deflect each time you hit 3+ excess here, adding fodder for Vi's legend exhaust next turn.

### Turn 5+ — Close through Yeti Brawler Gold economy and Xerath removal

Concrete line: Deploy Yeti Brawler (unl-018-219) for 6 energy. When it conquers with 3+ excess damage, two Gold gear tokens enter exhausted — each Gold token can be sacrificed and exhausted to add a rainbow rune next turn, fueling Hextech Gauntlets (unl-188-219) equip costs or splash rune requirements. Use Xerath - Freed's (unl-026-219) exhaust ability (1 Fury rune) to deal 3 damage to a specific unit at a battlefield, sniping annoying small blockers that prevent clean excess-damage conquers. Cast Right of Conquest (unl-015-219) — draw 1 plus 1 per battlefield you or allies control — to refill hand after the aggression.

Intended outcome: Hand is not empty despite five turns of aggression. Board is wide enough that the opponent cannot defend all three battlefields. If playing multiplayer FFA, Mageseeker Investigator (unl-163-219) taxes opponents who try to gang-pile your battlefield with multiple units simultaneously, protecting key conquests.

## Key conditional interactions (IF/THEN)

- IF you conquer a battlefield and assigned 3 or more excess damage (attacker total Might minus all defenders' combined Might is ≥ 3) THEN you may exhaust Vi - Piltover Enforcer (unl-187-219) to immediately ready one unit of your choice.
- IF a unit is readied by Vi's legend ability while it has Ganking THEN it may move to a different battlefield and attack again in the same turn — this is the primary "double-conquer" loop.
- IF Vault Breaker (unl-010-219) is cast on a unit before combat THEN that unit gains Assault 2 (+2 Might while attacking) and Ganking for the turn; combine with Vi - Destructive's existing Ganking to attack, conquer, move, and attack again.
- IF Inferna (unl-002-219) is held in hand while you already have a unit at a contested battlefield THEN you may play her as a Reaction (Ambush) at any point — including during your opponent's attack — adding her Assault 2 Might to the defense or reinforcing your own attack mid-combat.
- IF Lord Broadmane (unl-012-219) is played as an Ambush Reaction to a battlefield where you have units THEN all your other units at that battlefield gain Assault (+1 Might while attacking) for the turn — use to push a mass attack over the 3-excess threshold.
- IF Sharkling (unl-006-219) is played with its Accelerate cost (additional 1 energy + 1 Fury rune) THEN it enters ready instead of exhausted, allowing an immediate attack the turn it was played.
- IF Rengar - Unseen (unl-024-219) is chosen by an opponent's spell or ability THEN the opponent must pay an additional rainbow rune (Deflect tax) — this makes single-rune removal spells miss the timing or become too expensive.
- IF Rengar - Unseen is played with its Accelerate cost THEN he enters ready with Assault 2 + Deflect + Ganking, acting as an immediate repositioning threat that is difficult to answer.
- IF Vi - Hotheaded (unl-030-219) pays 2 energy + 1 Fury rune THEN her Might doubles for the turn — at 3 base Might she becomes 6, almost guaranteeing 3+ excess damage against most defenders and enabling the Vi legend trigger.
- IF Vi - Peacekeeper (unl-176-219) is played as an Ambush Reaction to a battlefield where you have units THEN she has Reaction timing AND when she attacks, she stuns one enemy unit (stunned unit deals no combat damage this turn) — use to neutralize the biggest blocker before swinging for excess damage.
- IF Upstage Comedy (unl-009-219) is cast for its Repeat cost (additional 2 energy) THEN two separate units are readied instead of one — chain with Vi's legend ability for three attacks in one turn.
- IF Square Up (unl-017-219) is cast with its Repeat cost (discard 1 card + 4 energy) THEN two units each gain Assault 4 for the turn — one unit can conquer while the other is held ready for Vi's legend exhaust.
- IF Towering Pairofant (unl-008-219) is played on a turn when any unit died THEN it enters ready automatically — play it after a trade, then use it as the fresh attacker for Vi's legend trigger.
- IF Yeti Brawler (unl-018-219) conquers a battlefield and assigned 3+ excess damage THEN two Gold gear tokens enter play exhausted; each Gold token can be sacrificed (kill it + exhaust) to add 1 rainbow rune — use to pay for Hextech Gauntlets equip, Upstage Comedy Repeat, or any splash-rune requirement.
- IF Trapping Grounds (unl-217-219) is your active battlefield and you conquer here with 3+ excess damage THEN a 1-Might Bird unit token with Deflect is generated — the Bird can be the "unit to ready" via Vi's legend exhaust, giving you a disposable attacker for subsequent turns.
- IF Carrion Dredger (unl-153-219) dies at a battlefield THEN a 1-Might Bird unit token with Deflect is played to your base (Deathknell trigger) — produces a blocker or future attacker even out of the trash.
- IF Valley of Idols (unl-218-219) is active and you play a unit there THEN you may pay 1 energy to Buff it (+1 Might if it doesn't already have a buff) — do this on Inferna, Sharkling, or any unit you intend to attack with, boosting their effective Might toward excess-damage thresholds.
- IF Xerath - Freed (unl-026-219) is stationed at a battlefield and you pay 1 Fury rune THEN exhaust him to deal 3 damage to any unit there — snipe a 3-Might blocker before attacking so your attacker faces zero resistance and scores maximum excess damage.
- IF Divining Shells (unl-161-219) is on the battlefield and you exhaust + sacrifice it THEN a unit you choose gains +2 Might this turn — use mid-combat to push an attacker over the 3-excess threshold after combat math has been declared.
- IF Tactical Retreat (unl-175-219) is held and a unit would die this turn THEN as a Reaction, heal it, exhaust it, and recall it to base — preserve your key attacker from an unfavorable trade, protecting investment in Hextech Gauntlets or Serrated Dirk equips.
- IF Mageseeker Investigator (unl-163-219) is stationed at a battlefield in multiplayer (FFA) THEN any opponent who tries to move multiple units simultaneously to that battlefield must pay a rainbow rune per unit beyond the first — this taxes gang-up strategies and protects a conquered battlefield from being retaken by a pile-on.
- IF Hextech Gauntlets (unl-188-219) targets a unit with high Might (e.g., 5+) THEN the 3-energy equip cost is reduced by that unit's Might — potentially making it 0 cost on Yeti Brawler (6 Might), granting a free permanent Might buff that synergizes with excess-damage math.
